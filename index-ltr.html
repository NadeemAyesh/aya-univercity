Smart Solutions to Support Gaza Reconstruction Efforts
Using Modern Geographic Technologies

The Department of Geography and Geographic Information Systems at the Islamic University of Gaza, in collaboration with Skipperfly, launches a global challenge for innovators and technologists to develop smart geographic solutions that address existing problems and improve spatial planning in the post-war phase, supporting reconstruction and sustainable recovery efforts in Gaza.

Our Vision

Amid the challenges facing the Gaza Strip, we transition from theoretical studies to digitally applicable field solutions. The competition aims to innovate technological tools that support reconstruction and sustainable recovery efforts.
It is an invitation to innovators and technologists worldwide to participate in building smart and sustainable tools that make a real difference in people's lives, transforming data into decisions, and technology into reconstruction based on sustainability and future resilience.

Competition Axes

Debris Management

This sector aims to develop innovative technological solutions using GIS and AI technologies for effective debris assessment and management. Solutions include creating tools for calculating debris volume, tracking collection sites, and analyzing its quality, contributing to solving disposal problems and improving the use of affected lands.

Water Sector

Smart models for tracking agriculture and water using remote sensing. This sector focuses on developing innovative tools for managing water resources in Gaza using remote sensing (RS) and GIS technologies. By tracking water sources and analyzing their flows, effective solutions can be provided to ensure water sustainability and reduce losses, contributing to solving water scarcity problems and securing supplies in affected areas.

Solid Waste Sector

This sector aims to provide innovative solutions for solid waste management using advanced GIS tools. By tracking waste flows and analyzing collection sites, collection and sorting mechanisms can be improved, helping to solve ongoing pollution problems and enhancing environmental sustainability in affected areas.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the competition and what are its objectives?

The competition is a global challenge aimed at developing smart geographic solutions using GIS and AI technologies to support reconstruction efforts in Gaza. The goal is to find innovative and effective solutions to address existing problems, improve spatial planning, and enhance decision-making in the post-war phase in Gaza.

How can I register for the competition?

You can register through the competition website via the "Join the Competition" button available on the homepage. Make sure to complete all required information and select one axis to participate in the competition.

What are the competition dates?

The competition starts on Tuesday, January 13, 2026.
The final deadline for project submission is Saturday, February 7, 2026.

Where can I submit projects?

You can click on the "Submit Project" button, noting that the button will be activated one week after the competition is published.

Can individuals or teams from all over the world participate?

Yes, participation is open to individuals and teams from all over the world, with a special invitation to graduates and engineers from Gaza in the fields of GIS, remote sensing, artificial intelligence, and data science.

Are there terms and requirements for submitting projects?

Yes, participants must integrate GIS technologies with AI to enhance results efficiency. The solution must be applicable in the context of Gaza and include interactive maps, Dashboards, and methodology explanation (see the competition terms section on the homepage).

Will base layers for Gaza be provided to participants?

Yes, you can benefit from the base layers (Base Layers) for Gaza to facilitate work and achieve the best results, by clicking on the following link: Link will be added here / Aya

Who are the members of the judging committee?

The judging committee consists of international experts in each sector separately (debris management, solid waste, water) to evaluate projects according to the scientific and technical standards of the competition.

What prizes will be awarded?

Cash prizes will be awarded to the top places ($1000 for first place, $700 for second place, and $400 for third place), in addition to consolation prizes and certificates of appreciation for the best projects.

Can I modify or add details to my project after submission?

No, once the project is submitted, submissions will be locked and cannot be modified. Make sure all details are complete before submitting the final project.

About the Islamic University and the Department of Geography and Geographic Information Systems

The Islamic University of Gaza is a leading academic institution in Gaza, distinguished by providing educational and research programs in various specializations. The Department of Geography and Geographic Information Systems in the Faculty of Arts at the university seeks to enhance scientific research and provide innovative solutions to environmental and spatial problems using advanced technologies such as GIS and remote sensing. The department is concerned with training students and providing applied studies in urban planning, natural resource management, and geographic data analysis.

About Skipperfly Company

Skipperfly is an international company specializing in Geographic Information Systems (GIS) technologies and artificial intelligence applications. The company contributes to the development of innovative technological solutions that support various sectors, and provides consulting and technical services for GIS applications in reconstruction and development projects around the world. Skipperfly's team is distinguished by its deep expertise in integrating advanced technologies to solve multiple problems and achieve improvements in reconstruction and sustainability operations.
