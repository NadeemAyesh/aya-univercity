Smart Solutions to Support Reconstruction Efforts in Gaza
Using Modern Geospatial Technologies

The Department of Geography and GIS at the Islamic University of Gaza, in cooperation with Skipperfly and RAED IT SERVICES, launches a global challenge for innovators and technologists to develop smart geospatial solutions that contribute to solving existing problems and improving spatial planning in the post-war phase, supporting reconstruction, recovery, and emergency rapid intervention in Gaza.

Our Vision

In light of the challenges facing the Gaza Strip, we are moving from theoretical studies to field-applicable digital solutions. The competition aims to innovate technical tools that support reconstruction and sustainable recovery efforts.
It is a call to innovators and technologists worldwide to participate in building smart and sustainable tools that make a real difference in people's lives, turning data into decisions and technology into reconstruction based on sustainability and future resilience.

Competition Tracks

Debris Management

This sector aims to develop innovative technological solutions using GIS and AI techniques to effectively inventory and process debris. Solutions include creating tools to calculate debris volume, track accumulation sites, and analyze its quality, contributing to solving disposal problems and improving the use of affected land.

Food and Water Security Management

This sector aims to develop smart models for managing water and food resources using Remote Sensing (RS) and GIS, focusing on agriculture tracking and water management in Gaza. Through these tools, water sources are monitored, flows are analyzed, and effective solutions are applied to preserve water resources and reduce loss. In light of current crises, this sector emerges as a key to solving water scarcity and serves as a rapid emergency intervention to ensure immediate supplies in affected areas.

Solid Waste Sector

This sector moves towards providing innovative solutions for solid waste management using advanced GIS tools. By tracking waste flows and analyzing accumulation sites, collection and sorting mechanisms can be improved, helping to solve ongoing pollution problems and enhancing environmental sustainability in affected areas.

Camp Management and Temporary Housing Planning

This axis focuses on developing innovative solutions for camp management and temporary housing planning in Gaza, using modern technologies to improve the living environment for displaced persons and affected communities. This includes planning and designing temporary residential spaces, improving basic infrastructure in camps, and managing resource distribution effectively to restore stability.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the competition and what are its goals?

The competition is a global challenge aiming to develop smart geospatial solutions using GIS and AI technologies to support reconstruction efforts and emergency rapid intervention in the Gaza Strip. The goal is to find innovative and effective solutions to address existing problems, improve spatial planning, and enhance decision-making in the post-war phase in Gaza.

How can I register for the competition?

You can register through the competition website via the "Register in the Competition" button available on the homepage. Ensure you complete all required data and choose one track to participate in.

What are the competition dates?

The competition starts on Tuesday, January 20, 2026.
The final deadline for project submission is Saturday, February 21, 2026.

Where do I submit the projects?

You can click on the "Submit Project" button, noting that the button will be activated one week after the competition announcement.

Can individuals or teams from all over the world participate?

Yes, participation is open to individuals and teams from all over the world, with a special invitation to graduates and engineers from the Gaza Strip in the fields of GIS, Remote Sensing, AI, and Data Science.

Are there conditions and requirements for project submission?

Yes, participants must integrate GIS technologies with AI to increase the efficiency of results. The solution must be applicable in the context of the Gaza Strip and include the submission of interactive maps, Dashboards, and an explanation of the methodology (refer to the competition terms section on the homepage).

Will base layers for the Gaza Strip be provided to participants?

Yes, you can benefit from the base layers for the Gaza Strip to facilitate work and achieve the best results by clicking the following link: Link here

Who are the jury members?

The jury consists of international experts in each respective sector (Debris Management, Solid Waste, Water) to evaluate projects according to the scientific and technical standards of the competition.

What prizes will be awarded?

Cash prizes will be awarded to the top places ($1000 for 1st place, $700 for 2nd place, and $400 for 3rd place), in addition to consolation prizes and certificates of appreciation for the best projects.

Can I edit or add details to my project after submission?

No, once the project is submitted, submissions will be locked and cannot be edited. Ensure all details are complete before submitting the final project.

About the Islamic University and the Department of Geography and GIS

The Islamic University of Gaza is a leading academic institution in the Gaza Strip, distinguished by offering educational and research programs in various disciplines. The Department of Geography and GIS in the Faculty of Arts at the university seeks to enhance scientific research and provide innovative solutions to environmental and spatial problems using advanced technologies such as GIS and remote sensing. The department is concerned with training students and providing applied studies in urban planning, natural resource management, and geographic data analysis.

About Skipperfly

Skipperfly is an international company specialized in Geographic Information Systems (GIS) technologies and artificial intelligence applications. The company contributes to developing innovative technological solutions supporting various sectors and provides consulting and technical services for GIS applications in reconstruction and development projects worldwide. The Skipperfly team is distinguished by its deep expertise in integrating advanced technologies to solve complex problems and achieve improvements in reconstruction and sustainability processes.

About RAED IT SERVICES

RAED IT SERVICES is a company specialized in providing innovative solutions in the fields of Artificial Intelligence and Geographic Information Systems (GIS), aiming to support innovation and improve institutional efficiency. The company offers smart solutions that help organizations make data-driven strategic decisions. By employing advanced technologies, RAED IT SERVICES seeks to enhance institutional excellence and accelerate digital transformations.
